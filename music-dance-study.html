🎵 Origin of Music

40kYears ago — earliest musical instruments (archaeological evidence)
🦴Perforated bear femur found in Slovenia, 1995 — possible ancient flute
📜Staff notation developed before 16th century → precise composition
🏛Ancient Greece: music = education for mind & soul
⛪Medieval: Gregorian chant + secular troubadours
Darwin: music evolved like animal mating calls (attraction)

🎻 Western Art Music

🎼Symphony — full orchestra, multiple movements. Ex: Beethoven Symphony No. 5
🎹Sonata — solo/small ensemble, 2–4 movements. Italian: "sonare" = to sound. Ex: Waldstein
🎺Concerto — soloist + orchestra. Ex: Tchaikovsky Piano Concerto No. 1
🎭Opera — music + singing + staging + dance. Originated Italy, end of 16th c.
🩰Ballet — orchestral comp for dance. Ex: Swan Lake (Tchaikovsky)
KEY DIFF: Symphony=full orch | Concerto=soloist+orch | Sonata=solo/small group

🎺 The 4 Orchestra Families

🎻Strings — adds HARMONY. Violin (smallest), Viola (warmer), Cello (bassist, sit). Pluck or bow.
🎵Woodwinds — adds COLOR. Air column vibration. Piccolo (highest, bird), Flute (no reed), Clarinet (single reed), Oboe (double, sad), Bassoon (double, lowest)
🎺Brass — adds POWER. Buzz lips, metal tubing. Longer = lower. Valves change tone.
🥁Percussion — adds RHYTHM. Struck. Skin/wood/metal. Timpani, snare, xylophone, cymbals
Memory: S-W-B-P → Strings-Woodwinds-Brass-Percussion → "Some Weird Band Plays"

💃 Dance — Core Elements

1Body — basic component; internal emotions expressed outward
2Theme — the message of the dance
3Design — plan/organization of movement in time & space
4aSpace — direction, levels, pathways, formations
4bTime — rhythm, tempo, duration
4cForce/Dynamics — strong, soft, sharp, flowing
5Technique — skill of executing movement
6Music — motivates movement
7Costume/Props — enhances; reflects culture
8Choreography — forms and arrangement
9Scenery — setting/background

🕺 Dance Types

🩰Ballet — Italian Renaissance 15c, precise/graceful, en pointe, turned-out legs
💃Ballroom — French courts late 16c, partner dance (waltz, foxtrot, tango, cha-cha)
🌀Contemporary — mid-20c, fluid/improv, Martha Graham & Merce Cunningham
🎤Hip Hop — 1970s Bronx NY, breaking/popping/locking, African American & Latino
🎷Jazz Dance — 17c African traditions → US 20c, improvisation, call-and-response
👟Tap — US 19c, African rhythm + Irish step, metal plates on shoes
🌺Folk — community heritage, rituals, festivals. Ex: Bharatanatyam, Samba, Hula
🍀Irish — stiff upper body, intricate footwork, Riverdance, pre-Christian roots
🌊Modern — early 20c reaction to ballet; Isadora Duncan, Martha Graham
🎶Swing — 1920s-30s US, Lindy Hop, Charleston, Jitterbug, Balboa

⚔️ Spanish Period (1521–1898)

✝️Missionaries used Gregorian chant, polyphonic music, Western notation
🎶Pasyon — Catholic teachings + indigenous music (liturgical)
🎸Harana — courtship serenade with guitar
💔Kundiman — slow, emotional; love/longing/patriotism
🪕Rondalla — string ensemble (plucked instruments)
🎭Zarzuela — Spanish-derived; sung + spoken scenes, 1–5 acts; arrived PH 1878
💃Cariñosa & Pandanggo sa Ilaw — localized European dances
🎵Kumintang — earlier style that influenced Kundiman

🇺🇸 American Period (1898–1946)

🏫Music added to public school curriculum
📻Radio, musical films, Bodabil (Filipino vaudeville)
🥁Philippine Constabulary Band (Walter Loving) — 1904 St. Louis World's Fair
🎓Conservatories established; Nicanor Abelardo & Francisco Santiago — nationalist composers
🕺Ragtime, foxtrot, early jazz in dance halls & cabarets
🇯🇵 Japanese Period (1941–1945)
🚫American influences BANNED; Japanese songs broadcast & taught
💣War devastation limited musical development; least lasting impact
📢Music used mainly for propaganda/political influence

🕊️ Liberation & New Society

POST-WAR LIBERATION (1946+)
🇵🇭Nationalism & cultural consciousness; swing revival; bodabil
🎸1950s-60s: samba → cumbachero bands; rock & roll, Elvis, The Beatles
🏛More music schools; RA 4723 — promoted music education
MARCOS / NEW SOCIETY (1972–)
📺Martial Law: strict media control BUT Imelda supported arts; built CCP
✊Bayan Ko (José Corazón de Jesús) — protest/patriotism; Benigno Aquino funeral
🎤Freddie Aguilar — "Anak" (international hit); Apo Hiking Society

🎵 Contemporary Philippine Music

📻Manila Sound (1970s) — Hotdog & VST & Co.; light pop-rock with Taglish
🇵🇭OPM & Pinoy Rock — Filipino identity in songwriting; Ryan Cayabyab
🤘Juan de la Cruz Band — pioneered Pinoy rock
🏆Metro Manila Popular Music Festival (Metro Pop), Himig Awards
⭐1980s–2000s: Regine Velasquez, Gary Valenciano, Eraserheads, Rivermaya
💬Social issues: Tatsulok (Bamboo) — inequality; Paraiso (Smokey Mountain) — environment
🆕Recent: P-Pop, indie music, hip-hop communities; global recognition

💃 Philippine Dance History

🌴Pre-Colonial: Sagayan Dance (Maguindanao/Maranao, honors Prince Bantugan); Igorot nature-inspired dances; passed orally
🕌Islamic (Mindanao): 12c; Singkil (Maranao court dance, bamboo poles, kulintang); elaborate costumes
⚔️Spanish: waltz, fandango, polka → localized → Maria Clara dances (from Noli Me Tangere character); Cariñosa
🌾Rural/Barrio: Pandanggo sa Ilaw (oil lamps, imitates fireflies); Sayaw sa Salakot; props = bamboo, candles, hats
🐦Tinikling — NATIONAL folk dance; from Leyte; mimics tikling bird; bamboo poles; symbolizes agility + harmony with nature

🧠 Memory Tricks — Memorize These!

🎺 4 Orchestra Families

S-W-B-P → "Some Weird Band Plays"
Strings · Woodwinds · Brass · Percussion

Their roles: Strings = Harmony, Woodwinds = Color, Brass = Power, Percussion = Rhythm
→ "Harmony Colors Bring Power Rhythmically" (alternate)

🎵 Woodwind Instruments (low to high pitch)

B-O-C-F-P → "Buy Our Cool Flutes Please"
Bassoon (lowest) · Oboe · Clarinet · Flute · Piccolo (highest)

Reed types: Bassoon=double, Oboe=double(sad), Clarinet=single, Flute=none, Piccolo=none (mimics birds 🐦)

🎻 String Family Size (small to big)

V-V-C-D → "Very Vibrant Cellos Dominate"
Violin (smallest, highest) · Viola (warmer) · Cello (bass, must sit) · Double Bass (biggest, lowest)

🎵 Symphony vs Concerto vs Sonata

Orchestra → One Soloist → One Solo
Symphony = full ORCHESTRA (all together) | Concerto = highlighted SOLOIST + orchestra | Sonata = solo/small group (most intimate)

Size: Symphony > Concerto > Sonata. All follow sonata form structure.
